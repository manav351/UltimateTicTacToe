Instructions

Each small 3 × 3 tic-tac-toe board is referred to as a Inner board, and the larger 3 × 3 board is referred to as the Outer board.
  1. The game starts with X playing wherever they want in any of the 81 empty spots. This move "sends" their opponent to its relative location.
  2. The next player has play in the inner board highlighted. The concept is that the outer board and inner board are mapped. The previous player move governs the location of the next player's board.
  3. If a move is played so that it is to win a local board by the rules of normal tic-tac-toe, then the entire local board is marked as a victory for the player in the global board i.e., the marker on the global board for the winner player is made.
  4. Once a local board is won by a player or it is filled completely, no more moves may be played in that board. If a player is sent to such a board, then that player may play in any other board i.e., the player can play corresponding to any available spots in 81 total spots.
  5. Game play ends when either a player wins the global board or there are no legal moves remaining, in which case the game is a draw.
